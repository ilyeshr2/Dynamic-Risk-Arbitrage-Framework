Strategy Overview

Core Setup

The strategy focuses on momentum scalping in the NQ (Nasdaq Futures) market, utilizing the 15-second chart timeframe to capture short-term momentum moves.

Target: 10 points per trade

Stop Loss: 10 points per trade

Risk:Reward Ratio: 1:1

Timeframe

Trading exclusively on the 15-second chart for NQ futures.

Trading Window: 9:30 AM - 11:00 AM ET (NY Session open)

Reason: Captures micro-movements with high precision during the most volatile and liquid market hours.

Entry Criteria

Signal Generation

  1. EMA Cross Confirmation: 21 EMA and 9 EMA crossover signals potential trend direction
  2. Break of Structure (BoS): Wait for price to confirm the new trend direction with a clear BoS
  3. Pullback: Enter on the second pullback after BoS, typically when price retests the EMA levels
  4. Wick Test Alternative: Recently added alternative entry on valid wick tests of swing highs/lows

Market Conditions Filter

  • Avoid trading when there are price gaps (signals low volume and potential choppiness)
  • Do not enter trades if the EMA cross occurred before market open
  • Wait until after major economic announcements (minimum 5 minutes)
  • Only trade during high volume and volatility (9:30-11:00 AM ET)
Critical Rule: Never enter two consecutive trades in the same trend direction after being stopped out.

Daily Loss Limits

Phase 1 ($800)

Maximum daily loss: 4 trades or $100 (12.5% of account)

This conservative approach provides psychological comfort during the initial phase.

Later Phases

As account grows, daily loss limit remains at 4 consecutive trades or 10% of account value, whichever is smaller.

After 3 consecutive losses, take a mandatory 10-minute break to reset mentally before continuing.

Key Principle: Consistent 2.5% risk per trade across all phases maintains disciplined position sizing as account grows.

Trading Psychology

Psychological Challenges

  • Fear of Drawdowns: Identified as the primary psychological barrier
  • Overtrading: Tendency to enter additional trades after losses
  • Position Sizing Discipline: Temptation to increase size after losses

Mental Approach

  • Patience: "Be patient, be patient, and when the opportunity comes, don't be afraid"
  • Process Over Outcome: Focus on executing the strategy correctly, not on P&L
  • Accepting Drawdowns: Understanding that drawdowns are normal operating costs, not failures

Trading Rules

Rule #1: Consistent Position Sizing

Never increase position size after losses. Maintain the 2.5% risk per trade rule at all times.

Rule #2: No Re-entering Same Trend

Never enter a second trade in the same trend direction after being stopped out.

Rule #3: Pre-Market EMA Cross

Never enter a trade if the EMA cross occurred before market open, as it doesn't represent NY session potential trend.

Rule #4: Mental Reset

After 3 consecutive losses, take a mandatory 10-minute break to reset mentally before continuing.
